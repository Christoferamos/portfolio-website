App Bemol

App Bemol began to be reformulated in mid-August 2020 with the aim of bring a better experience to customers in its main functions, such as purchase, payment and relationship of these customers with the Bemol brand.

Company

Bemol

Type

Product design

Year

2020

Process

The Bemol app is a direct channel between Bemol and customers, where it is it is possible to perform some of the most important actions found on the site such as purchases, payment of installments, and balance check.

Impact of COVID-19 Pandemic

Given the emergency nature of the project, most of the stages of discovery were set aside in favor of gaining speed, based on team members' market experience for the construction of the app.

‍

At a certain point in the deliveries, it was decided between Product Designers, Product Owner and Product Managers, to test the Bemol app product information disposal for having a different proposal from retail market.

Testing

The test was structured in a phase, containing 3 tasks with different objectives, including evaluating first impressions, discover the most used means of finding a product and review the product sheet.

Test Structure Artifacts:
- Test Plan
- Script containing the tasks and the interview
- App in production environment with distribution
controlled

Plan and script were elaborated between the Product Designers of BU (Business Unit) with the approval of the artifacts by the PO.

Outcome

What went right: Bemol Identity preserved

The majority of test participants gave positive feedback on the visual aspect of the app, saying that "looks like Bemol", in addition to having found more "clean" and modern. As for the product screen, all participants liked "by demand", being possible to access the desired information within "drawers".

Action plan: Prioritization of improvement points

After pointing out the participants' problems and the observers' perceptions during the tests, a prioritization dynamic was carried out with the purpose of listing the most critical items to prepare action plans.

But what went wrong anyway?

Despite the focus being on the product sheet, the search for the requested item in the script showed that we need to improve the search engine, whether due to accessibility or taxonomy aspects, most of the participants had difficulty finding the requested product with the expected assertiveness.

Launch

For the launch, we managed to implement the following improvements: Redesign the product card considering the longest possible name, Redesign a card with tags containing the product attributes (classification, etc.) and Include an "Add" button in the 'product card' component.
